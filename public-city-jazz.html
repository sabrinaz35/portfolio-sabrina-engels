Public city jazz

Information

My role: Visual designer

Individual assignment

Duration: period of 3 weeks

Subject: Visual design, year 2 CMD

Assignemt

For this course, we were expected to create a digital onepager using the rotterdam style, but also an attractive interface for young people interested in jazz.

In this course I was introduced to the principle design system and we have been working on different button states. And making a user friendly attractive user interface.

Goal

The goal was to analyze and apply visual indentity in which a message is communicated in the interface. In addition, the aim was also to make the design responsive.

Proces

During the process of this interface, I took several key steps to arrive at a final design.

Visual analysis/h3>

First, I had done some good research on what elements in the Rotterdam style design system I could start using and what could also make the interface visually appealing.

Lo-fi designs

After exploring the possibilities in design, I made some sketches.

First variant hi-fi

After I made the sketches I picked a design and worked it out.

Using a grid, I aligned everything. This was for a grid of four columns.

I had received feedback on this and the very question asked was “Is this attractive for the target audience to go to?” Since I myself fell within the target audience, I actually answered no immediately.

In addition, I had based the figures on the artists of the bands that would be playing during the event.

Scroll to see the whole onepager :)

Hi-fi version

In the end, I did maagen the basics of the first design, but tried to focus more on making it more attractive.

In doing so, we were instructed to think what kind of feeling jazz gave us and what keywords you associated with it. In my case, it was happy, colourful and chill.

Based on those key words, I started trying and pretty quickly this idea came out of that.
